# Competitive Landscape: Security Ratings / Cyber Risk Ratings

In the Forrester Wave: Cybersecurity Risk Ratings Platforms, Q2 2024, Forrester scored 10 vendors; **SecurityScorecard, Bitsight, and Panorays were named Leaders**, with Black Kite and Recorded Future among the Strong Performers. SecurityScorecard publicized having the highest Current Offering score of the 10; Bitsight noted its third consecutive Leader placement. Caveat: most head-to-head comparison pages below are vendor-authored and adversarial by design.

## Bitsight

Closest peer and historical rival. Ratings on a 250–900 scale (credit-score styling), daily updates. Moody's invested $250M in 2021 at a $2.4B valuation, becoming its largest shareholder; Bitsight simultaneously acquired VisibleRisk and has since been embedded across Moody's risk products (TechCrunch; Bitsight/Moody's). Strengths vs. SSC: perceived rigor/analytics, the Marsh McLennan correlation study, and deep traction in cyber insurance and financial services. Weaknesses cited by competitors: pricing that scales steeply with monitored entities, and historically thinner native TPRM workflow (questionnaires/remediation) requiring add-on modules (UpGuard competitor analysis).

## UpGuard

Positions as "complete cyber risk management" for lean teams: ratings plus native vendor-assessment workflow, breach/leak detection, and claims full vendor rescans every 24 hours vs. SecurityScorecard's staggered scan cycles. Its published critiques of SSC lean on IP-attribution complaints and scan-freshness. Counterpoint: UpGuard's own data breadth, threat-intel depth, and enterprise integrations are considered lighter than SSC/Bitsight (vendor-authored comparisons; treat directionally).

## Panorays

Differentiates on combining external ratings with automated, contextualized questionnaire workflows and "Risk DNA" per-relationship scoring; named a Leader in the 2024 Wave. Smaller footprint than SSC/Bitsight; strong in mid-market TPRM programs where questionnaire automation matters as much as the rating (Panorays/Forrester materials).

## Black Kite

OSINT-based, non-intrusive scans presented in three lenses: a technical letter grade, probable financial impact using the Open FAIR model, and compliance-framework correlation. Marketed as deeper "cyber risk intelligence" including ransomware-susceptibility and Nth-party analysis. Forrester rated it a Strong Performer in 2024; competitors argue it lacks full TPRM workflow (Black Kite and UpGuard comparison pages).

## RiskRecon (Mastercard)

Acquired by Mastercard in 2020; known for asset-level attribution quality claims and "risk priority matrix" (issue severity x asset value). As of July 2026 there is **no public sunset/discontinuation announcement we could verify** — the product remains marketed, was named in Gartner's 2025 TPRM Market Guide, and Mastercard is actively integrating it with Recorded Future (acquired for $2.65B, closed Q1 2025) and a Feb 2026 Cloudflare data partnership that produces A–F ratings (BankInfoSecurity; American Banker). Rumors of a RiskRecon wind-down: (unverified). Strategically, Mastercard's center of gravity has shifted toward Recorded Future threat intelligence, creating uncertainty competitors exploit.

## OneTrust and GRC-side adjacents

OneTrust (also ProcessUnity, Prevalent, Archer) attack the problem from the workflow/GRC side: vendor inventories, assessments, contract and compliance management — typically ingesting ratings feeds from SSC/Bitsight partners rather than producing proprietary ratings. They compete for the same TPRM budget, arguing ratings are a data input, not a program.

## Where SecurityScorecard is considered strong / weak

- **Strong**: instantly legible A–F grades for boards/executives; free self-monitoring tier driving broad adoption; breadth of the rated universe; Forrester's top Current Offering score in 2024; expansion into managed services (MAX), questionnaire automation (HyperComply), and internet scanning (Driftnet).
- **Weak (as cited by rivals and users)**: IP/asset misattribution and false positives requiring disputes; staggered scan cadence vs. daily-rescan claims from UpGuard; opaque custom pricing; and the general category criticism that outside-in scores miss internal controls (see ratings-industry-criticism.md).
